Opponents in this game are categorized not by hit dice or level but by their **role in a scene**. A handful of bandits and a primordial horror are not built on the same scale, and they should not run on the same rules. The system uses four broad categories, each with its own stat-block shape and the gamemaster expense it costs to deploy them.

## Creature categories

| Category       | Role                                         | Stat block | Typical Doom cost to deploy |
| -------------- | -------------------------------------------- | ---------- | --------------------------- |
| **Minion**     | Mook. Forgettable. Dies easily.              | Tiny       | Free in a scene; reinforcements cost 1–2 |
| **Toughened** | Named threat, individually competent.        | Full       | 1–3 to bring in mid-scene   |
| **Nemesis**    | Major villain; a recurring problem.          | Full + signatures | 3–5 to bring in mid-scene |
| **Horror**     | Sorcerous, otherworldly, or inhuman in scale. | Full + horror traits | 3–5; usually scene-defining |

### Minion

A Minion has:

- **One attribute value** used for everything (e.g. 9).
- **One or two skill Expertise values** (e.g. *Melee 1*).
- **No Focus.**
- **Vigor or Resolve equal to a small number** (1–3); typically loses Vigor in flat amounts rather than dice.
- **No Reactions** unless they spend Doom to take one.

Minions are **mowed down** by even modest hits. A Minion is removed from the scene the first time it takes any meaningful Wound. They exist to be threatening in number, not individually.

A useful gamemaster shortcut: pool a squad of minions into a single token at the table. A "squad of four" has Vigor 4 and one Melee attack; reduce its count as it takes damage.

### Toughened

A Toughened opponent has a full stat block — every relevant attribute, Expertise, Focus, talents, gear, and the standard Vigor / Resolve tracks. They are essentially other characters, just on the opposing side of the screen.

Toughened opponents:

- Take Wounds and Traumas like player characters.
- Get one **free Reaction per round** (Parry, Dodge, or equivalent).
- Are the natural opposition for a single scene: the captain of the watch, the priest-magician, the bandit chieftain.

### Nemesis

A Nemesis is a Toughened with **a signature feature** — a unique talent, a named weapon, a personal patron — and the gamemaster is encouraged to *spend Doom freely* to keep them alive. A Nemesis:

- Has the same statline shape as Toughened.
- Has 1–2 *signature talents* that name the character ("the Whispered Curse of Saadan," "the Black Reaver's Stance").
- Should **escape** the first time they would die, given any plausible exit. Pay Doom to make this happen. They are a problem the heroes will see again.

### Horror

A Horror is something *other* — a thing whose biology, motivation, or physics aren't shared with the people fighting it. Mechanically:

- Full statline, often with one or two extreme attribute values (Brawn 16, Willpower 14).
- One or more **Horror traits**: *Fearsome X* on attacks; immunity to certain mundane damage; an aura that imposes Resolve costs on anyone within Close.
- May *regenerate* or *recur* in ways a mortal opponent cannot.

A Horror should change the *terms* of the encounter, not just the difficulty.

## Reading a stat block

A representative full stat block uses this shape. Use it for Toughened, Nemesis, and Horror entries.

<div class="stat-block">
  <h4>Example Toughened — Brigand Captain</h4>
  <p class="meta">Toughened · Mortal Foe</p>
  <p><strong>Attributes:</strong> Agility 10 · Awareness 9 · Brawn 11 · Coordination 9 · Intelligence 8 · Personality 10 · Willpower 9</p>
  <p><strong>Fields of Expertise:</strong> Combat 2 (Focus 1) · Movement 1 · Social 1 (Focus 1) · Senses 1 · Knowledge 0 · Fortitude 1</p>
  <p><strong>Vigor:</strong> 11 · <strong>Resolve:</strong> 9 · <strong>Soak:</strong> 3 (hardened leather) / Courage 3</p>
  <p><strong>Attacks:</strong> Broadsword (Melee, 4 [CD], 1H, Parrying, Vicious 1) · Throwing knife (Ranged Close, 2 [CD], Hidden 2, Thrown)</p>
  <p><strong>Special Abilities:</strong> <em>Cruel.</em> The Captain may spend 1 Doom to add a Trauma alongside any Wound they inflict.</p>
  <p><strong>Doom Spends:</strong> 2 Doom — call in 2 Minion brigands from the surrounding terrain. 3 Doom — turn the fight into a chase across the rooftops, switching scene type.</p>
</div>

The shape, expanded:

- **Category line.** Minion / Toughened / Nemesis / Horror, plus a flavor tag.
- **Attributes.** The full seven.
- **Fields of Expertise.** A shorthand grouping of related skills — used in place of listing every skill separately. The Fields are roughly:
  - **Combat** (Melee, Ranged Weapons, Parry)
  - **Movement** (Acrobatics, Athletics, Stealth)
  - **Senses** (Awareness, Insight, Observation, Survival)
  - **Social** (Command, Counsel, Persuade, Society)
  - **Knowledge** (Alchemy, Craft, Healing, Linguistics, Lore, Warfare)
  - **Fortitude** (Discipline, Resistance, Sailing, Animal Handling)
- **Vigor / Resolve / Soak.** Standard tracks.
- **Attacks.** Each named with damage and Qualities.
- **Special Abilities.** Signature talents and traits.
- **Doom Spends.** Pre-priced things this opponent can do when the gamemaster reaches for the pool — saves you from improvising mid-fight.

## Building encounters on the fly

A useful budget for a typical scene with four players:

| Encounter strength       | Composition                                              |
| ------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Easy**                 | 2–3 Minion squads, no Toughened. Heroes resolve quickly. |
| **Standard**             | 1 Toughened + 2 Minion squads.                           |
| **Hard**                 | 1 Toughened + 3 Minion squads, or 2 Toughened + 1 squad. |
| **Nemesis encounter**    | 1 Nemesis + 1 Toughened + 1 squad.                       |
| **Horror encounter**     | 1 Horror, often alone. The terrain is the second enemy.  |

Mix the layers when you can. A scene with only Minions is forgettable; a scene with only the Horror is intense but flat. The most memorable fights have one big problem and a half-dozen smaller ones biting at the heroes' ankles.

## Generic stat-block templates

Four bare-bones templates you can drop in without prep. Attribute values assume a "standard" mortal opponent; adjust upward by 1–2 for elites.

### Generic Minion

- **All attributes** 8.
- **Combat 1** (no Focus).
- **Vigor** 3. **Resolve** 3. **Soak** 1 (leather scraps).
- **One attack** at 3 [CD].

### Generic Brigand (Toughened, Mortal)

- Agility 10 · Awareness 9 · Brawn 10 · Coordination 9 · Intelligence 8 · Personality 9 · Willpower 9
- Fields: Combat 2 (F1) · Movement 1 · Senses 1 · Social 1 · Knowledge 0 · Fortitude 1
- Vigor 10 · Resolve 9 · Soak 3
- Sword (4 [CD], Parrying, Vicious 1) · Sling (2 [CD], Medium)

### Generic Sorcerer (Toughened, Mortal)

- Agility 8 · Awareness 10 · Brawn 7 · Coordination 8 · Intelligence 12 · Personality 10 · Willpower 13
- Fields: Combat 0 · Movement 0 · Senses 2 (F1) · Social 1 · Knowledge 3 (F2) · Fortitude 2 (F1)
- Vigor 7 · Resolve 13 · Soak 0 / Courage 4
- Curse Touch (Sorcery, Intense, 3 [CD]) · Patron — *spend Doom to add 1 [CD] to any sorcery damage*.

### Generic Wild Beast (Toughened, Beast)

- Agility 10 · Awareness 11 · Brawn 12 · Coordination 9 · Intelligence 4 · Personality 7 · Willpower 9
- Fields: Combat 2 · Movement 2 (F1) · Senses 2 (F1) · Social 0 · Knowledge 0 · Fortitude 2
- Vigor 12 · Resolve 9 · Soak 2 (hide)
- Bite (4 [CD], Vicious 1, Grappling) · Charge (Knockdown, 1 zone)

## Quick reference

- **Four categories:** Minion · Toughened · Nemesis · Horror.
- **Minions** die fast and are deployed in pools; **Toughened** are full characters.
- **Nemesis** opponents should *escape* their first death-by-Doom.
- **Horrors** change the terms, not just the difficulty.
- **Each Toughened+ block** lists Attributes, Fields, Vigor/Resolve/Soak, Attacks, Specials, and pre-priced Doom Spends.
