When the dice need to decide who hits whom and in what order, the game switches into an **action scene**. Action scenes are organized into rounds; each round, every participant acts once. A round represents a few seconds of fictional time — enough for a single deliberate attempt at something significant.

## Turn order

There is no initiative roll. Instead, action scenes use **player-led turn order**:

1. At the start of each round, the players collectively decide which of them acts first. They may not act twice in a row — once a player has acted, the next slot defaults to the gamemaster.
2. After each player's turn, the gamemaster takes a turn with one of their non-player characters. The gamemaster picks which.
3. The gamemaster may **seize the initiative** by spending **1 Doom**, taking a turn out of order before the next player goes.
4. Once every participant on both sides has acted, the round ends. New round, new pick.

This makes the order of battle a tactical question: the players pick whose turn it is, and that choice itself is part of how the scene plays. A wounded hero might step up first to break free of an enemy; a heavy hitter might hold for last to clean up.

## Distances and zones

The system does not use squares or measured feet. Distances are abstracted into a small ladder of **zones**:

| Zone     | Description                                                                                |
| -------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Reach**   | Within arm's length. Melee range. Two characters at Reach occupy the same point.        |
| **Close**   | A few steps away. One quick stride and you are at Reach.                                |
| **Medium**  | Across a room or a clearing. A full move to close.                                      |
| **Long**    | Distant but visible. Multiple moves to close; thrown weapons strain at this range.      |
| **Extreme** | At the edge of useful action. Bows only; difficult terrain to cross.                    |

Zone abstraction lets the gamemaster narrate terrain quickly: "the hall is a Medium zone; the dais where the sorcerer stands is a Close cluster on the far side."

## What you can do on your turn

Each character may take, in any order:

- One **Standard Action** (most attacks, most spells, most significant tasks).
- Any number of **Minor Actions** that fit the moment, with the second and beyond costing 1 Momentum/Doom each.
- One **Movement** — a shift one zone closer or further, or repositioning within the current zone.
- One **Reaction** during another participant's turn (see below).

Common Minor Actions: drawing a weapon, calling out to an ally, kicking over a table, picking something up. The gamemaster has discretion to demote a small physical thing to "free" (no Momentum cost, no action) when it would be silly to charge for it — and to promote something more involved to a Standard Action.

## Movement

A single move shifts the character **one zone** in any direction. Difficult terrain (rubble, a swaying ship, a chest-deep river) may demand a skill test (often *Athletics* or *Acrobatics*) to cross, or cost an additional movement.

- **Sprint:** spend 1 Momentum to move a second zone in the same turn.
- **Run:** dedicate the whole turn to crossing distance — move up to **three zones**, but you may not also take a Standard Action.
- **Difficult terrain:** the zone counts as if it were one step further away; a skill test or a Momentum spend ignores the penalty.

## Reactions

A character may take **one Reaction per round**, off their turn, in response to a trigger:

- **Parry** (Coordination + Parry) against an incoming melee attack.
- **Dodge** (Coordination + Acrobatics) against an incoming attack.
- **Counter-spell** (Willpower + Sorcery) against an incoming spell.
- **Protect** an adjacent ally — interpose yourself, taking the attack in their stead.
- **Free-form** — any narrative response the gamemaster will permit ("I dive for the rope as the bridge collapses").

A character can take a *second* Reaction in the same round by spending **1 Momentum** (or the gamemaster spending 1 Doom). The pool runs dry quickly when a participant tries to defend against every blow.

## Surprise

If one side has clearly ambushed the other, the surprised side **cannot take Reactions** during the first round, and gets no turn until at least one of their attackers has acted. The gamemaster decides whether a surprise applies; an *Observation* or *Insight* test may negate it for an alert character.

## Ending the scene

An action scene ends when:

- All hostile participants are out of the fight (down, dead, fled, or yielded).
- The objective that started the scene is resolved (you escaped, the bomb went off, dawn came).
- Both sides choose to stop fighting.

Once the scene ends, banked Momentum **drains entirely**. Doom does not — it follows the gamemaster through the rest of the session.

## Quick reference

- **Turn order:** players choose, alternating with the gamemaster.
- **Zones:** Reach · Close · Medium · Long · Extreme.
- **Per turn:** 1 Standard, 1 Movement, 1+ Minors, 1 Reaction off-turn.
- **Sprint** = +1 zone for 1 Momentum. **Run** = 3 zones, no Standard Action.
- **Seize initiative** for the GM: 1 Doom.
