Momentum and Doom are the two opposing economies that give 2d20 its distinctive tempo. Momentum is what heroes spend to make good things happen; Doom is what the world spends to make bad things happen. Each is generated by play and each is paid back into play. A session of Conan tends to live or die by how aggressively both pools are being used.

## Momentum

Momentum is the surplus of a successful test. Every success beyond the difficulty becomes 1 Momentum.

- A successful D1 test rolling 3 successes generates **2 Momentum**.
- A successful D3 test rolling 5 successes generates **2 Momentum**.

The character may **spend** Momentum immediately, or **bank** it to a shared group pool for later use. The group pool caps at **6**, and **1 Momentum drains from the pool at the end of each round** of an action scene (or each scene in narrative time).

### Spending Momentum

Momentum buys you more of whatever just happened — the success gets bigger, faster, broader, or louder. Common spends:

| Spend                                            | Cost |
| ------------------------------------------------ | ---- |
| Buy 1 bonus d20 (max +3 dice on a test)          | 1 / 2 / 3 |
| Reduce the difficulty of an ally's test by 1     | 1    |
| Re-roll your own dice pool on a test             | 1    |
| Add 1 [CD] of damage to an attack                | 1    |
| Disarm, knock prone, push, or other "secondary"  | 2    |
| Get an additional question answered on a success | 1    |
| Take a second Minor Action this turn             | 1    |
| Add a fact / detail to the scene                 | 2    |
| Heal 1 [CD] of Vigor or Resolve                  | 1    |

The full list is open-ended — the gamemaster can price an unusual spend on the fly. The rule of thumb: **1 Momentum buys a modest improvement, 2 buys a bigger one, 3 buys a major one.**

### Generating Momentum without rolling

A few sources generate Momentum outside the test economy:

- Some talents.
- A successful **assist** that overflows your needs.
- Certain weapons and items.
- Gamemaster fiat for excellent narration.

### Banking and decay

Banked Momentum belongs to the *group*, not the individual. Anyone may spend from it. Because it drains, hoarding is rarely correct — if you have 4 in the bank and it's the end of the round, you've lost 1 by inaction.

## Doom

Doom is Momentum's negative reflection — the gamemaster's pool. It represents the world tightening around the heroes: reinforcements, ambushes, sorcerous interference, bad weather, worse luck.

### How Doom is generated

The pool grows whenever the heroes choose convenience now in exchange for trouble later. Common sources:

| Source                                                          | Doom |
| --------------------------------------------------------------- | ---- |
| A player buys a bonus d20 with Doom instead of Momentum         | 1 / 2 / 3 |
| A player accepts a *complication* to keep a success             | 2    |
| A player invokes a special talent that costs Doom               | varies |
| A scene shifts in the heroes' favor through implausibly good luck | gm sets |
| A natural 20 on a test (the gamemaster may bank the complication as Doom rather than spend it now) | 1 |

The gamemaster may also seed Doom at the start of dangerous scenes — usually 1 per player character — to represent the scene's inherent threat.

### How Doom is spent

The gamemaster spends Doom to act against the heroes:

| Spend                                                        | Cost |
| ------------------------------------------------------------ | ---- |
| Buy a bonus d20 for an enemy's test                          | 1 / 2 / 3 |
| Add 1 [CD] of damage to an enemy attack                      | 1    |
| Activate a special effect on an enemy attack or trap         | 1–3  |
| Move an enemy as a bonus action / give an enemy a Reaction   | 1–2  |
| Bring in reinforcements (a minion squad or a single Toughened) | 1–3 |
| Bring in something far worse (Nemesis, Horror, named villain) | 3–5 |
| Inflict an environmental complication (a torch dies, ground gives way) | 1–2 |
| Seize the initiative — an enemy acts before any hero who has not yet acted | 1 |

### Doom never decays

Unlike Momentum, Doom is permanent until spent. The gamemaster can bank it across scenes and turn the screws when the moment is right.

## The push and pull

The interesting decisions in 2d20 happen when a player must choose between **paying with Momentum** (a cost paid out of the team's near-term capacity) and **paying with Doom** (a cost paid by the future). Both can be the right call. The character who spends Doom for a third bonus die is mortgaging the next ten minutes of play in exchange for getting through this one wall. Sometimes that's exactly correct. Sometimes it's how a TPK starts.

For the gamemaster, the equivalent question is *when* to spend Doom. A Doom pool that sits at 8 all session is a wasted resource; a Doom pool that empties at the climax is what makes the climax feel like a climax. Spend down to zero with intent.

  <p class="callout-title">A common mistake</p>

## Quick reference

- **1 success past the difficulty = 1 Momentum.**
- **Bank caps at 6**, drains 1 per round/scene.
- **Doom never drains.**
- **Player-to-GM conversions:** complication accepted = +2 Doom. Bonus die bought from Doom = 1/2/3.
- **Spend Momentum every round.**
