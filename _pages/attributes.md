Every character is described by seven attributes. They are the raw stuff the skill tests are built on — every roll combines an attribute with a skill, and the attribute changes depending on what the character is trying to do with that skill. A character can swing a sword for raw force (*Brawn + Melee*) or for finesse (*Agility + Melee*); the same skill, a different attribute.

Attributes for player characters typically range from **7** (poor) to **13** (exceptional), with **9** being roughly competent. Heroic characters at the top of the world might reach 14 or 15 in a single attribute.

## The seven attributes

| Attribute        | What it measures                                                                                                            |
| ---------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Agility**      | Quickness, dexterity, fine motor control. Sneaking, fighting with finesse, balancing on a ship's rigging.                   |
| **Awareness**    | Perception, intuition, attention to the world. Spotting an ambush, reading a stranger, listening for the wrong silence.     |
| **Brawn**        | Raw physical power and endurance. Smashing doors, wrestling beasts, shrugging off wounds.                                   |
| **Coordination** | Hand-eye precision under pressure. Archery, throwing, ship-handling, surgery.                                               |
| **Intelligence** | Reasoning, memory, learned knowledge. Languages, lore, planning, craft.                                                     |
| **Personality** | Force of presence and charm. Leadership, deceit, seduction, command of beasts.                                              |
| **Willpower**    | Self-mastery and conviction. Resisting fear, sustaining concentration, working sorcery without losing one's grip on sanity. |

## Which attribute, which skill?

Each skill has a default attribute that pairs with it, listed in [Skills & Focus]({{ '/skills/' | relative_url }}). The gamemaster can override the default when the fiction makes another attribute more appropriate. A few common re-pairings:

- **Melee** is normally *Agility*, but is *Brawn* for a great-axe charge or *Willpower* for a desperate last stand.
- **Persuade** is normally *Personality*, but is *Awareness* when reading what an audience needs to hear, or *Intelligence* when constructing a long argument.
- **Stealth** is normally *Agility*, but is *Awareness* when the trick is knowing where the guard isn't looking, or *Willpower* when holding still inches from discovery.

The principle: pick the attribute that names *what the character is leaning on* in this moment. Skill measures what they know how to do; attribute measures the part of themselves they are bringing to bear.

## Attribute use outside skill tests

A handful of derived values come straight off attributes:

- **Vigor** (physical hit pool) = *Brawn*.
- **Resolve** (mental hit pool) = *Willpower*.
- **Defense — Parry** is based on *Coordination* plus a defensive skill or a shield.
- **Defense — Dodge** is based on *Coordination* plus a defensive skill or armor characteristics.
- **Soak** comes off armor; **Courage** comes off Willpower and certain talents.

## Changing attributes

Attributes change rarely. Story-driven loss (a sorcerous curse, a maimed hand) can lower an attribute; sustained training or transcendent reward can raise one. The default state is stability — a character's seven numbers are who they are, and growth happens at the skill, talent, and equipment layers.

## Quick reference

- **Range**: 7–13 for PCs, 14+ for exceptional heroes.
- **Skill TN** = attribute + skill Expertise.
- **Vigor** = Brawn. **Resolve** = Willpower.
- **Default pairings** can be overridden when the fiction warrants.
