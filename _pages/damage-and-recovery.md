Heroes carry two parallel hit tracks. **Vigor** absorbs physical damage; **Resolve** absorbs mental damage. They are not pools you bleed to zero before falling — they are the soft layer that takes the abuse before something that *matters* gets hurt.

## Vigor and Resolve

| Track       | Equals        | Restored by                                      |
| ----------- | ------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| **Vigor**   | Brawn         | First Aid, rest, recovery tests between scenes.  |
| **Resolve** | Willpower     | Rallying words, downtime, dealing with the cause. |

Damage rolled past **Soak** (armor for physical, *Courage* for mental) reduces the appropriate track. When the track hits **0**, the next damage inflicts a **Wound** (physical) or **Trauma** (mental) instead.

## Wounds and Traumas

A Wound is a serious physical injury — a deep cut, a broken rib, a stab through the leg. A Trauma is its mental counterpart — a moment of horror, a shaken conviction, a thread of madness creeping in.

When you would take damage and your relevant track is at 0:

1. **Take 1 Wound or Trauma**, regardless of how much damage was rolled.
2. Reset your Vigor or Resolve to its full value (it represents the new equilibrium of the now-injured character — not "you're healed," but "you're back to functional with an injury").
3. Some attacks (Vicious, called shots, criticals) impose a Wound on the *first* damage that gets through, even before the track is exhausted.

Characters can stack Wounds and Traumas. The thresholds:

| Wounds (or Traumas) | Effect                                                                       |
| ------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **1**               | Step up the difficulty of physical (or mental) tests by 1 while the Wound persists. |
| **2**               | As above; each subsequent test costs 1 Momentum or Doom to attempt at all.   |
| **3+**              | The character is **dying** (or **broken**). See below.                       |

## Dying and broken

A character at 3 Wounds is *dying* — they remain in play but unable to take Standard Actions, and another character must stabilize them within a small number of rounds (default **3**) or they die. Stabilization is a *Healing* test (D2) and may also require gear (bandages, splints).

A character at 3 Traumas is *broken* — they remain in play but their player should describe how the character is no longer the person they were a moment ago. A *Discipline* test (D2) by an ally or by the character themselves can stabilize the mind. Long-term recovery from Traumas often requires downtime narrative scenes, not a roll.

## Soak: armor and courage

**Soak** is the static reduction applied to incoming damage. Two flavors:

- **Armor Soak** reduces incoming physical damage. Each armor entry lists its rating. Heavy armor has the side cost of imposing penalties on Athletics, Acrobatics, and Stealth tests.
- **Courage** reduces incoming mental damage. Default Courage equals **Willpower / 3**, rounded down. Talents and items can raise it.

Soak is applied **once per attack**, after damage is rolled. Multiple sources of Soak generally **do not stack** — take the higher value.

## Recovery — in a scene

Limited and expensive:

| Method            | Action                | Effect                                        |
| ----------------- | --------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| **First Aid**     | Standard, *Healing* D1+ | Restore Vigor [CD] equal to successes.      |
| **Patch up**      | Minor + 1 Momentum    | Restore 1 [CD] of Vigor to an ally.           |
| **Rallying word** | Standard, *Command* D1+ | Restore Resolve [CD] equal to successes.    |
| **Catch breath**  | Spend 1 Fortune       | Restore Vigor OR Resolve to full.             |

Healing with First Aid only works **once per scene per character** — the first attempt is a triage, the second isn't going to do anything useful in the middle of a fight.

## Recovery — between scenes

Far more generous. Between scenes (and given a few minutes of safety):

- Each character recovers **all Vigor** and **all Resolve**.
- Each character may attempt a *Healing* (Intelligence) or *Discipline* (Willpower) test to remove **1 Wound or 1 Trauma**. Difficulty is **D2**; an injured comrade with the right gear can assist.
- Conditions like *Prone*, *Restrained*, *Dazed* end at scene's close unless they have a persistent cause.

## Recovery — between adventures

Long downtime (days to weeks). Characters:

- Heal all remaining Wounds and Traumas, barring those that are narratively permanent (a missing eye, a scar of madness).
- Spend Gold on living and on personal projects.
- Train new skills, talents, or spells (see [Character Creation]({{ '/character-creation/' | relative_url }}) for XP costs).

## Fortune

Fortune points are the system's "luck and grit" currency:

| Fortune spend             | Effect                                                                |
| ------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Add 1d20 to a test**    | The d20 automatically scores 1 success (no roll).                     |
| **Catch breath**          | Restore Vigor OR Resolve to full immediately.                         |
| **Story declaration**     | Introduce a useful fact to the scene ("I bribed this guard last week"). |
| **Cheat death**           | Survive what should have killed you, on the gamemaster's terms.       |
| **Inspired action**       | Take a second Standard Action this turn.                              |

Most characters start with **3 Fortune**, capped at 5. Fortune refreshes between adventures. Use it — saved Fortune at the end of a campaign is wasted Fortune.

## Quick reference

- Vigor = Brawn; Resolve = Willpower.
- 0 Vigor/Resolve → next hit causes a **Wound** / **Trauma**, then track resets.
- 3 Wounds = dying; 3 Traumas = broken.
- Soak reduces damage once per attack; armor and Courage are the two kinds.
- Full track recovery between scenes; Wounds with a D2 *Healing* test; everything resets between adventures.
- Fortune is the panic button — spend, don't hoard.
