Characters are built through a sequence of ten short steps, each adding a layer of identity and a small mechanical package. The procedure is *life-path*: you discover who your character is by walking them from their birthplace, through their formative experiences, into the moment they pick up the sword and begin their first adventure.

The full prose of each homeland, caste, archetype, and story should be read in the rulebook — that's the part that gives the system its character. This page describes only the mechanical structure so you can prep a character quickly once you've picked your flavor.

## The ten steps

1. **Homeland.** Pick where the character was born. Each homeland grants:
   - A bonus to one attribute (typically +1).
   - One *homeland talent*.
   - One *homeland skill* (Expertise +1).
   - A starting language and any cultural details that should appear on the sheet.
2. **Attributes.** Distribute attribute points across the seven attributes. Starting characters typically end the step with attributes in the **7–11** range, with the homeland bonus already applied. Two common approaches:
   - **Array:** assign the fixed array (e.g. 10, 10, 9, 9, 8, 8, 7) before homeland bonus.
   - **Point-buy:** spend a budget of points, with each attribute starting at 7.
3. **Caste.** Pick the character's social rank. Caste grants:
   - One *caste talent*.
   - One *caste skill* (Expertise +1).
   - A *standing* with that caste's institutions.
   - A typical starting income and accommodation.
4. **Story.** Pick or roll a personal story — the singular event that defines the character's youth (a feud, a vision, a betrayal). Story grants:
   - +1 Expertise to a relevant skill.
   - A trait or contact tied to the story.
5. **Archetype.** Pick what the character is *for*: the warrior, the scholar, the thief, the priest, the soldier, and so on. Archetype grants:
   - +1 to a relevant attribute.
   - Two skill Expertise points and one Focus point in archetype-relevant skills.
   - One *archetype talent*.
   - A signature item or piece of starting equipment.
6. **Nature.** Pick a defining personality trait — a single descriptor that captures how the character moves through the world (proud, curious, cruel, devout). Nature grants:
   - One skill Expertise improvement.
   - Occasional bonuses or complications when the trait is leaned into during play.
7. **Education.** Pick how the character learned what they know: street, monastery, military, mentor, self-taught, etc. Education grants:
   - One skill Expertise + Focus improvement.
   - An additional language or lore specialty.
8. **War Story.** Pick the formative *adventure* — the first scrape that made the character a hero, even before the campaign begins. War Story grants:
   - One skill Focus improvement.
   - A scar, debt, or enmity worth noting on the sheet.
9. **Finishing Touches.** Pick:
   - Name, age, appearance, and any defining mannerism.
   - A *belief* or *code* the character holds, which the gamemaster can lean on for hooks.
   - One additional language, contact, or piece of gear within reason.
10. **Final Calculations.** Compute derived values:
    - **Vigor** = Brawn.
    - **Resolve** = Willpower.
    - **Soak** from armor (physical) and from talents/willpower (mental, *Courage*).
    - **Defenses** — Parry and Dodge values, computed from Coordination plus the appropriate skill and gear.
    - **Fortune Points** — usually 3 at start, capped at 5 unless a talent raises it.
    - **Starting Gold** and possessions tallied from caste, story, and archetype.

## Random vs. directed creation

Each step has a roll-on-it table for players who'd rather discover their character than design them. Mixing modes is fine — roll homeland and story, pick archetype and nature, and let the dice surprise you on the rest.

A useful default for a first session: roll the first three steps (homeland, attributes if using array, caste), then pick deliberately for the rest. Random homeland produces unexpected pairings — a Stygian acrobat, a Cimmerian scholar — that tend to be more interesting at the table than the obvious archetype-driven character.

## Alternate creation modes

Tables that want to move fast can use:

- **Pre-generated characters** drawn from the rulebook's roster.
- **Archetype quickstart** — pick an archetype and apply a pre-built package, then bolt on a homeland for flavor.
- **Lifepath compression** — combine Story, Nature, Education, and War Story into a single "background" pick worth four packaged Expertise points and one talent.

## Starting resources

Most characters begin play with:

- **3 Fortune** (max 5 by default).
- **Vigor** = Brawn; **Resolve** = Willpower.
- Starting equipment from archetype + one personal item.
- Starting **Gold** by caste (handful for low-caste, a respectable purse for high-caste, no money but rich obligations for noble).
