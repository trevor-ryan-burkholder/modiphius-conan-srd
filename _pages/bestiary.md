A working bestiary of generic foes — mortal, animal, monstrous, and otherworldly. Each entry is a template: rename, restat, or repaint to fit the scene. For the stat-block format see [Encounters → Reading a stat block]({{ '/encounters/' | relative_url }}#reading-a-stat-block).

Categories follow the four creature tiers from the Encounters page: **Minion** · **Toughened** · **Nemesis** · **Horror**.

Where a Field of Expertise is omitted, treat it as 0 with no Focus.

## Building a custom opponent quickly

A useful five-step shortcut for inventing a foe at the table:

1. **Pick a tier.** Minion / Toughened / Nemesis / Horror. This drives the stat-block size.
2. **Pick a body plan.** Mortal / Beast / Undead / Otherworldly. This tells you what Qualities and resistances apply.
3. **Pick an attack signature.** One weapon or natural attack with two Qualities. Damage range: 2 [CD] (Minion) → 4 [CD] (Toughened) → 6 [CD] (Nemesis) → 7+ [CD] (Horror).
4. **Pick a single trick.** A Doom Spend, a talent, a horror trait — *one*, not three. The opponent's personality at the table comes from that single trick.
5. **Set Vigor / Resolve.** Roughly: Brawn for Vigor, Willpower for Resolve, with the gamemaster nudging up or down by 2.

That gets you to "playable" in about thirty seconds.

## Quick reference

- **Minion** = remove on first Wound; **Toughened** = full PC-style tracks; **Nemesis** = should *escape* their first death by Doom; **Horror** = changes the rules, not just the numbers.
- **Beast** template: high Awareness, Movement, and Senses; low Intelligence/Social.
- **Horror** template: Fearsome, often immune to a category of mundane damage, may regenerate or vanish.
- **Doom Spends** are pre-priced things the opponent can do — write them on the stat-block to save improvising mid-fight.
