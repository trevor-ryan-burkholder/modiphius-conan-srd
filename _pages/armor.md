## Helms

A helm is its own piece, worn alongside body armor. The Soak from a helm only applies against attacks that target the head (called shots, certain creature attacks).

| Helm               | Bonus | Cost | Qualities                                      |
| ------------------ | ----- | ---- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| Coif (mail or padded) | +1 vs head | 3 | —                                          |
| Open helm (skullcap) | +1 vs head | 5 | —                                          |
| Closed helm        | +2 vs head | 12 | –1d20 on *Observation*, *Persuade*           |
| Great helm         | +3 vs head | 25 | –1d20 on *Observation*, *Persuade*; Encumbering |

If a body armor entry doesn't specify, assume the wearer is unhelmed.

## Shields

A shield is wielded in the off-hand alongside a 1H weapon (or instead of one). It grants both:

- The ability to make a **Parry** Reaction without a separate parrying weapon.
- A bonus to the **difficulty** of attacks made against the wielder, while the shield is borne.

| Shield             | Parry bonus | Cost | Qualities                                |
| ------------------ | ----------- | ---- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Buckler            | +1d20 on Parry | 4 | Hidden 1                                  |
| Round shield       | +1 difficulty to incoming attacks | 6 | —                            |
| Heater / kite shield | +1 difficulty; +1 vs ranged | 10 | Encumbering                      |
| Tower shield       | +1 difficulty; +2 vs ranged | 18 | Encumbering 2; act as light cover |
| Spiked shield      | +1 difficulty; shield bash 2 [CD] Knockdown | 12 | —                       |

A bash with a shield uses *Melee* and treats the shield as an improvised 1H weapon (2 [CD], Knockdown). With training, treat the bash as not Improvised.

## Armor qualities

- **Encumbering X.** Add X to the difficulty of *Athletics*, *Acrobatics*, and *Stealth* tests while worn.
- **Noisy.** *Stealth* tests at +1 difficulty regardless of other modifiers.
- **Constraining.** Caps the *Focus* contribution to *Acrobatics* at 0; you cannot benefit from your Focus on tumbling, climbing flips, etc.
- **Cumbersome.** Cannot be worn while swimming without an automatic *Resistance* (D3) per round to stay afloat.

Multiple armor entries do **not** combine — a single body armor and one helm and one shield, at most. The benefit of wearing two layers of mail is, in this system, a memory of the second layer's smell, not extra Soak.

## Maintenance and damage

A long campaign should occasionally bite armor:

- Each scene in which the wearer takes a Wound, the armor loses 1 Soak until repaired.
- A *Craft* (D2) test during downtime restores 2 Soak per success.
- Armor reduced to Soak 0 is broken and must be reforged (cost: equal to the armor's value).
- Magical and exotic armor may resist damage; gamemaster's call.

## Adapting to setting

Some armor is rare or prohibited in particular regions — a full plate harness on a northern raid is as out of place as a fur cloak on a desert march. The gamemaster may rule that certain regions have:

- **Cultural restrictions.** Mail is normal in one place, taboo in another.
- **Climate restrictions.** Full plate in a jungle imposes a *Resistance* test each scene against heat exhaustion.
- **Economic ceilings.** A village smith may not sell anything above Soak 4, no matter what you offer in coin.

## Quick reference

- **Soak** is the static reduction applied once per attack.
- **Heavier armor = better Soak**, but stacks more **Encumbrance**.
- **Helms** add +1 to +3 Soak against called shots to the head.
- **Shields** add a Parry option and raise incoming attack difficulty.
- **Multiple armors do not stack** — wear one body, one helm, one shield.
