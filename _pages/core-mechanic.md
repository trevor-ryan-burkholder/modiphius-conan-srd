The 2d20 system, as its name suggests, is built around rolling a pair of twenty-sided dice against a target number and counting how many of them succeed. Almost everything else in the game — the spending of Momentum, the threat of Doom, the shape of combat — extends from this single procedure.

## The skill test

When a character attempts something with a meaningful chance of failure, the gamemaster calls for a skill test. The procedure is:

1. The gamemaster picks the **skill** (e.g. *Melee*, *Persuade*, *Stealth*) and the **attribute** that supports it (e.g. *Agility*, *Personality*, *Awareness*) for this specific attempt.
2. The character's **target number** is the sum of that attribute and that skill's *Expertise*.
3. The gamemaster sets a **difficulty** — the number of successes required, usually D1 (one success) but as high as D5 for the truly desperate.
4. The player rolls two d20s. Each die that rolls equal to or under the target number scores one **success**.
5. If the character has *Focus* in that skill, any die that rolls equal to or under the Focus value scores **two** successes instead of one.
6. A natural **1** is always a critical success — it counts as two successes regardless of Focus.
7. A natural **20** is a **complication** — see below.

If the number of successes meets or exceeds the difficulty, the test succeeds. Any successes beyond the difficulty are converted into **Momentum**.

## Difficulty

Most actions are Difficulty 1 (D1). The full ladder:

| Difficulty | Description                                                            |
| ---------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **D0**     | Trivial. No test required — narrate success unless it would be boring. |
| **D1**     | Average. The default for most uncertain actions.                       |
| **D2**     | Challenging. Specialized work, awkward conditions.                     |
| **D3**     | Daunting. Heroes routinely fail this without help.                     |
| **D4**     | Dire. Only the most capable have any chance.                           |
| **D5**     | Epic. Possible only with significant aid, talents, or Fortune.         |

## Bonus dice

Characters can buy extra d20s for a single test, up to **three** additional dice (five total). The cost rises sharply:

| Extra d20s | Cost (in Momentum / Doom, or other source) |
| ---------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| +1         | 1                                          |
| +2         | 2                                          |
| +3         | 3                                          |

Each bonus die is rolled and read the same way as the originals. Bonus dice may also come from teamwork, talents, or special equipment.

## Reading the dice — worked example

A character with **Agility 9** and **Stealth (Expertise 2, Focus 1)** is sneaking past a guard. Target number is **9 + 2 = 11**. Focus is **1**.

The player rolls a 4 and a 17.

- The 4 is under the target (success). It is also under the Focus value of 1? No — 4 is greater than 1, so it scores **one success**, not two.
- The 17 is over the target — no success.

That is **one success**. If the gamemaster set this at D1, the character slips past. If D2, they have failed and the gamemaster narrates the consequence (likely spending Doom in the process).

Had the player rolled a **1** and an 11: the 1 is a critical (2 successes); the 11 is exactly the target (1 success). Total **3 successes** against D1 — they pass and bank **2 Momentum**.

## Critical successes and complications

- **Natural 1**: a critical, worth 2 successes always.
- **Natural 20**: a complication. If the test still succeeds, the gamemaster narrates a wrinkle — a torch goes out, a weapon is damaged, a witness appears. If the test fails, the complication compounds the failure. Each additional natural 20 on the same roll adds another complication.

A player may always **accept** rather than narrate a complication by adding 2 Doom to the gamemaster's pool instead. Used judiciously this lets a player keep a vital outcome ("but I do reach the rope!") at a price they choose to pay.

## Combat Dice (Challenge Dice)

Damage and many secondary effects use a special six-sided die. Read its faces as follows:

| Face | Result                                                              |
| ---- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1    | 1 damage                                                            |
| 2    | 2 damage                                                            |
| 3, 4 | 0 damage                                                            |
| 5, 6 | 1 damage *and* triggers any **Effect** the weapon or attack carries |

So an attack rolling 4 of these dice ("4 [CD]") averages around 4 damage and has a roughly 56% chance per die of triggering at least one Effect.

## Quick reference

- **Target number** = Attribute + Skill Expertise.
- **Successes per die** = 1 under TN; 2 under Focus; 2 on a natural 1.
- **Default test** = 2d20 vs D1. Bonus dice cost rises 1, 2, 3.
- **Natural 20** = complication.
- **Excess successes** = Momentum (banked or spent immediately).
