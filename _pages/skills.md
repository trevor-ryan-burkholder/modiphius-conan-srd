Each skill has two numbers attached: **Expertise** (added to the attribute to form the target number) and **Focus** (the threshold at or below which a die scores two successes instead of one). Most starting characters have Expertise 1–3 and Focus 0–2 in the skills they care about, with 0/0 in the rest.

Where a skill page says "(Agility)" beside the name, that is the *default* attribute. The gamemaster can substitute another when the action calls for it (see [Attributes]({{ '/attributes/' | relative_url }})).

## Expertise and Focus

**Expertise** broadens *what counts as success*. A higher target number means more of the d20's range scores at all. Raising Expertise from 1 to 3 turns a TN of 10 into a TN of 12 — modest in isolation, large over a campaign.

**Focus** sharpens *how good your successes are*. A high Focus means more of your hits crit. Focus is what makes a specialist — a 9/3 melee fighter scores far more big hits than a 12/0 one, even though their TN is the same.

In general: raise Expertise to widen your reach into new fields; raise Focus to deepen your mastery of the few skills your character is *for*.

## Untrained tests

A character with Expertise 0 in a skill can still attempt the test — they roll against attribute alone. The gamemaster may impose a +1 difficulty step on untrained tests for anything that requires real training (sorcery, surgery, ship-handling), or rule it impossible outright (one does not stumble through casting an enchantment without ever having studied).

## Assisting another character

Up to two characters can **assist** the lead character on a test, each rolling 1d20 using their own attribute + skill. The assisting dice are added to the lead's pool. Each assistant may suffer their own complications on a natural 20. Assistance only works if the helpers can plausibly contribute.

## Group tests

When many characters attempt the same thing together (a stealthy approach by a whole party, a forced march), each player rolls. The test succeeds if **half or more** of the rolls succeed. Excess successes from each contributor still generate Momentum.

## Extended tests

For long projects — picking a complex lock, ritual sorcery, mending a sundered hull at sea — the gamemaster sets a **Work** value and an **interval**. Each test the character rolls contributes its successes toward the Work total. The test may also have a **Difficulty** (per roll) and a **Progress Rate** cap that limits how much you can accomplish in one interval. Time runs out, monsters arrive, or the storm worsens if the work isn't done by a stated deadline.

## Quick reference

- **TN** = Attribute + Expertise.
- **Focus** turns dice ≤ Focus into 2 successes.
- **Untrained** = roll against attribute only; gamemaster may raise difficulty.
- **Assist** = up to 2 helpers, +1d20 each.
- **Group test** = succeeds if at least half the rolls succeed.
