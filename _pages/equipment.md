The Hyborian Age does not run on a magic-item economy. Wealth in this game is mostly *for spending*: feeding yourself, bribing officials, mending your sword, paying the wineshop, hiring a guide who knows which corner of the Stygian backcountry is fatal. Treasure left in the bank between adventures often evaporates between sessions, and that is by design.

## Gold and currency

Money is tracked simply, in **Gold**. The denomination represents whatever local coinage the gamemaster prefers; a Gold is "enough to comfortably feed a man for a day in a city, or to buy a passable blade in a country market."

A rough cost ladder:

| Item / service                                  | Cost (Gold) |
| ----------------------------------------------- | ----------- |
| Common meal, common drink                       | <1          |
| One night in a poor inn                         | 1           |
| One night in a respectable inn                  | 2–3         |
| Travel by cart, per day, between settlements    | 1           |
| Passage on a coastal ship, per day              | 2           |
| Common clothes, dagger, sling, bow              | 1–3         |
| Sword, decent quality                           | 5–8         |
| Mail or hauberk                                 | 10–20       |
| A trained horse                                 | 15–25       |
| A small fishing boat                            | 50–100      |
| Bribery — minor official                        | 1–5         |
| Bribery — captain of the watch                  | 25+         |
| Bribery — provincial governor                   | Whatever you have, and a favor |

The gamemaster sets prices to the local market — a sword in the Nordheim is dear, in Aquilonia merely expensive. Selling looted gear typically returns **half** the listed cost, less if the item is obviously stolen or hot.

## Belongings and encumbrance

Encumbrance is tracked qualitatively, not by weight. A character has:

- **Items always carried** — belt, purse, dagger, signet, the small handful of things they never put down. Free.
- **Loadout** — what is on the character's body and ready to use this scene: weapons drawn or sheathed, armor worn, a bow with a quiver, a few useful tools. Roughly five or six items, with the gamemaster's leave.
- **Pack / saddle / hold** — bulk goods that are *with* the character but not *on* their person. Accessing requires a Minor Action and reasonable circumstances ("I rummage in my pack").
- **Stash / shipboard / camp** — back at base; not accessible during a scene unless the scene is at base.

Heavy armor, big weapons, and bulky bags impose **Encumbered**, which steps up the difficulty of *Athletics*, *Acrobatics*, and *Stealth* tests by 1.

## Weapon properties (Qualities)

Each weapon entry lists a **base damage in [CD]**, a **size** (1H or 2H), a **range** if thrown or ranged, and one or more **Qualities**. The common Qualities:

| Quality              | Meaning                                                                              |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Parrying**         | Counts as a shield for *Parry* — usable to defend without a separate item.           |
| **Vicious X**        | +X damage per Effect rolled on the [CD].                                              |
| **Piercing X**       | Reduces target's Soak by X for this attack.                                          |
| **Knockdown**        | First Effect rolled knocks the target *Prone*.                                       |
| **Intense**          | Damage is delivered as mental damage (Resolve) instead of physical.                  |
| **Fearsome X**       | If the attack causes a Wound, also deal X [CD] mental damage.                        |
| **Reach**            | Treats Close-range targets as Reach for the attacker.                                |
| **Unbalanced**       | After using, no Parry Reaction until your next turn (heavy two-handed weapons).      |
| **Hidden X**         | Easy to conceal; *Observation* to spot is +X difficulty.                             |
| **Volley**           | Suitable for area attacks across formations.                                         |
| **Thrown**           | Designed to be thrown; uses *Ranged Weapons*.                                        |
| **Reload Minor / Std** | Requires a Minor or Standard Action to reload between shots.                       |
| **Improvised**       | Untrained weapon; +1 difficulty to attack, fragile.                                  |

## Armor properties

Armor is described by an **Armor Soak** (the static physical-damage reduction) and a **Qualities** list. Heavy armor brings encumbrance and noise; light armor brings little of either.

| Armor             | Soak | Qualities                                |
| ----------------- | ---- | ---------------------------------------- |
| No armor          | 0    | —                                        |
| Heavy clothes     | 1    | —                                        |
| Padded jack       | 2    | —                                        |
| Hardened leather  | 3    | —                                        |
| Brigandine        | 4    | Encumbering                              |
| Mail hauberk      | 5    | Encumbering, Noisy                       |
| Heavy mail        | 6    | Encumbering 2, Noisy                     |
| Scale or plate    | 7    | Encumbering 2, Noisy, Constraining       |

Armor qualities:

- **Encumbering X** — adds X to the difficulty of *Athletics*, *Acrobatics*, *Stealth* tests.
- **Noisy** — your *Stealth* tests are at +1 difficulty.
- **Constraining** — sets a hard cap on certain skills (e.g., max Focus contribution to *Acrobatics*).
- **Helm** — separate piece worn with most armor; +1 Soak vs called shots to the head, but you may suffer *Stealth/Observation* penalties.

A **shield** is worn alongside a 1H weapon and grants both improved Parry and the ability to make a Parry Reaction without spending a hand to draw.

## Quick reference

- Wealth is in **Gold**, sold at half, prices set to the local market.
- Encumbrance is **qualitative** — Loadout / Pack / Stash.
- **Weapon Qualities** modify how damage is read; **Vicious** and **Piercing** are the most common.
- **Armor Soak** reduces incoming physical damage once per hit. Heavy armor is *Encumbering*.
- A shield grants better Parry and frees a hand for it.
