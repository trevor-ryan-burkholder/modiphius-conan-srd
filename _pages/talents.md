Talents are the customization layer on top of skills. Where a skill says "you can fight with a sword," a talent says "you can sweep multiple foes when you do." Each skill in the system has its own small talent tree — typically a handful of starter talents and one or two more advanced ones that require a starter as a prerequisite.

This page does not list every talent in the rulebook. It describes the *structure* — how to read a talent block, how to acquire talents, and the common shapes you'll see again and again — so that the table can interpret a new talent on sight.

## How a talent is written

Each talent is presented in a small block with the following parts:

<div class="stat-block">
  <h4>Talent Name</h4>
  <p class="meta">Skill · Prerequisite (if any) · Maximum Ranks (if applicable)</p>
  <p><strong>Effect.</strong> The mechanical thing the talent does. May trigger automatically, on a specific roll, or when the character spends a resource (Fortune, Momentum, Doom).</p>
</div>

A few talents stack — they have a maximum rank greater than 1, and each rank either improves the magnitude of the effect or extends what it applies to.

## Acquiring talents

Characters begin play with a small set of talents granted by their archetype, caste, and story (see [Character Creation](/character-creation/)). Additional talents are bought with experience between sessions. The standard cost ladder:

| Talent type                  | XP cost |
| ---------------------------- | ------- |
| Skill talent (no prereq)     | 200     |
| Skill talent with prereq     | 400     |
| Sorcery spell                | 200     |
| New caste/homeland privilege | 400     |

You cannot purchase a talent whose prerequisite you do not yet meet. Some talents also require a minimum Expertise or Focus in the parent skill.

## Common talent shapes

Most talents you'll see fall into a few recognizable patterns. Learning the patterns makes new talents easy to read:

- **Re-rolls.** "You may reroll one d20 on a [skill] test." Powerful and clean. Reroll talents are usually once per scene.
- **Cost reduction.** "Buying bonus dice for a [skill] test costs 1 less Momentum/Doom (minimum 1)."
- **Difficulty reduction.** "Reduce the difficulty of [a specific kind of test] by 1."
- **Effect substitution.** "On a successful [skill] test, you may also achieve [secondary effect]." Common in combat: knock prone, disarm, etc.
- **Resource generation.** "When you do X, gain 1 Momentum / generate 1 of a special pool."
- **Combat-specific.** "Your attacks with [weapon type] gain Effect on a roll of 4–6 instead of 5–6." Or "Add +1 [CD] to your damage with [weapon type]."
- **Sorcery support.** Allow casting at reduced cost, with reduced risk, or with greater range/duration.

## Talent interactions

Several rules apply across all talents:

- **No double-dipping.** If two talents would apply the same modifier (e.g., both reduce a test's difficulty by 1), take only the larger benefit unless the talents explicitly stack.
- **Triggered talents are optional.** A talent that triggers on a condition (e.g., "when you take damage") is the character's choice to invoke; they may decline if they prefer not to spend the associated cost.
- **Per scene / per session.** Talents capped at a usage frequency reset at the start of the relevant interval. "Per scene" resets when the gamemaster declares a new scene; "per session" resets at the start of the next play session.

## Designing your own talents

Homebrew talents are encouraged; the framework is forgiving. A good homebrew talent:

1. Has a clear trigger ("when you do X")
2. Has a clear effect ("then Y happens")
3. Optionally has a cost (spend Momentum, raise Doom, expend a use/scene)
4. Belongs to a parent skill

A useful sanity check: compare it to an existing talent in the same skill family. If it is strictly better than that talent, it is probably too strong. If it is strictly weaker, it is dead weight.

## Quick reference

- Talents attach to skills, sometimes with a prerequisite chain.
- Most are bought with XP between sessions (200 / 400 typical).
- Read the block as **Skill · Prereq · Effect**.
- Same-source bonuses don't stack — take the larger.
