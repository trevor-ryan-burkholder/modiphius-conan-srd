This is a system reference document for running games in the Hyborian Age using the 2d20 system. It is a fan project, not an official publication, and is intended for use among a small group at the table — a quick-lookup companion to the published rulebook rather than a replacement for it. Where this document and the printed book disagree, trust the book.

The pages here cover the mechanical heart of the game: how the dice work, how characters are built, how Momentum and Doom drive the rhythm of a scene, how blows fall and wounds accumulate, how spells are cast and paid for. Setting material — the deep history of the Hyborian world, the gazetteer of its kingdoms, evocative descriptions of named characters and adventures — is deliberately left to the official sources, which do that work far better than a margin-note ever could.

## How to use this site

Use the sidebar to jump between chapters; each is a self-contained reference page. The order broadly follows the structure of a game session: who is at the table, what they roll, what their character can do, what happens when the swords come out, and what the gamemaster does behind the screen.

Most rules pages end with a short **Quick reference** block. The [Tables Appendix]({{ '/appendix-tables/' | relative_url }}) gathers the most commonly looked-up numbers in one place so you can scroll instead of flip.

## A note on scope

This document covers rules, not flavor. You will find skill names, dice procedures, talent effects, weapon properties, sorcery costs, and combat sequencing here. You will not find lengthy descriptions of the Hyborian kingdoms, named heroes and villains, or scenarios from the rulebook. Those belong to the source — read them in their original presentation, where the prose and the art carry the weight they were meant to.

If you have not yet bought the rulebook, do. This SRD is most useful as a way to stop flipping through 400 pages mid-fight, not as a substitute for owning the thing.
